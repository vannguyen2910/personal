Making it easier to find the right learning content, at scale

Go1 is a corporate learning content marketplace, aggregating thousands of courses from hundreds of providers. My focus was Search & Discovery and Content Curation — helping learners and admins find the right content faster, and giving the business a clearer read on what wasn't working.

The problem

With a catalogue spanning thousands of courses from hundreds of content providers, the core challenge wasn't a lack of content — it was findability. Learners and workplace admins needed a fast, trustworthy way to discover relevant content inside a genuinely large, heterogeneous library, and the team lacked a consistent, evidence-based read on where discovery was breaking down.

My approach

I ran a structured research effort to build that evidence base: 5+ surveys and 20+ interviews with learners and admins, aimed at understanding how people actually searched for and evaluated content, and where they lost confidence or gave up.

Alongside qualitative research, I helped define the key UX metrics for search and discovery and implemented Fullstory dashboards so the team could track behaviour continuously, not just at the point of a single study. I also built an Intercom feedback pipeline, applied across 5+ initiatives, so ongoing user feedback fed directly into design and product decisions rather than sitting in a backlog.

Design response

Working with the product team, I co-led a novel design pattern that used Generative AI to improve content discovery — moving beyond keyword search toward a more guided, intent-aware way of surfacing relevant content. I built a comprehensive prototype for usability testing, iterating the pattern against real user behaviour before it went further into delivery.
